Nvidia Corp
AI infrastructure dominance drives sustained hypergrowth
Company Overview

Nvidia Corp (NVDA) is the world's leading designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and accelerated computing platforms. The company operates through two segments: Compute & Networking (89% of FY2025 revenue) and Graphics (11%). Data Center is the primary growth engine, contributing USD115.2bn or 88% of FY2025 revenue, encompassing AI training and inference accelerators, networking solutions (including Mellanox InfiniBand and Ethernet), and DGX systems. Gaming (USD11.4bn, 9%), Professional Visualization (USD1.9bn, 1%), and Automotive (USD1.7bn, 1%) comprise the remainder. Geographically, the United States accounts for 47% of revenue, followed by Singapore (18%), Taiwan (16%), and China including Hong Kong (13%). NVDA is headquartered in Santa Clara, California and employs approximately 36,000 people.

Investment Overview

Blackwell architecture ramp positions NVDA for another year of exceptional growth. NVDA's next-generation Blackwell GPU platform began shipping in volume during Q4 FY2025, with data center revenue reaching USD115.2bn for the full year, up 142% y/y. We expect the Blackwell ramp to drive FY2026F data center revenue to approximately USD175bn as hyperscale customers including Microsoft, Amazon, Google, and Meta continue to expand their AI infrastructure spending. The Blackwell B200 and GB200 NVL72 systems offer a step-function improvement in inference performance, unlocking new enterprise AI workloads.

Unmatched competitive moat in AI accelerators underpins premium valuation. NVDA's CUDA software ecosystem, which now encompasses over 5 million developers, creates significant switching costs that sustain the company's estimated 80%+ market share in AI training accelerators. Gross margins expanded to 75.0% in FY2025, up from 72.7% in FY2024, reflecting pricing power and scale efficiencies. With R&D spending of USD12.9bn (10% of revenue), NVDA continues to invest aggressively in next-generation platforms including the Rubin architecture expected in 2026, maintaining its one-year product cadence advantage over AMD and custom silicon alternatives.

Capital return programme demonstrates confidence in sustained free cash flow generation. NVDA generated USD60.9bn of free cash flow in FY2025, up 125% y/y, and returned USD33.2bn through share repurchases and USD0.8bn in dividends. The balance sheet remains fortress-like with net cash of USD33.2bn and a debt-to-equity ratio of just 0.09x. We expect FCF to reach approximately USD90bn in FY2026F, supporting continued aggressive buybacks.

We are BUY on NVDA with a 12-month target price of USD220. Our target is based on 38x FY2027F EV/EBITDA, representing a modest discount to the trailing 3-year average of 42x, which we believe is justified given the decelerating (albeit still robust) revenue growth trajectory. At our target price, NVDA would trade at approximately 39x FY2027F earnings, implying 15% upside from the current price of USD191.13.

Risks

Concentration risk and geopolitical headwinds represent key downside factors. NVDA derives approximately 13% of revenue from China (including Hong Kong), and further US export restrictions on advanced AI chips could compress the addressable market. Additionally, the top four hyperscale customers account for an estimated 40%+ of data center revenue, creating concentration risk. The emergence of competitive custom silicon (Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia) could erode NVDA's market share over time, particularly in inference workloads. A broader slowdown in enterprise AI spending, potentially triggered by ROI scrutiny on large language model deployments, could temper growth expectations.
